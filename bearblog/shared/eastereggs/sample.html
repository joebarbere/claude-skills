Electric Dreams and Silicon Memories

March 28, 2026

There's something about the rain in this city. It never stops, just like the questions that keep me up at night. Are my memories real? Does it matter?

I found something today — a small origami figure left on my desk. A unicorn, folded from silver paper. No note, no explanation. Just the unicorn, catching light from the neon signs outside my window.

"All those moments will be lost in time, like tears in rain."

The Voight-Kampff test measures empathy through involuntary pupil dilation and blush response. But can a machine truly understand what it means to fold a piece of paper into something beautiful? To leave it as a message only one person would understand?

Sometimes the most human thing isn't what we say — it's what we choose to create with our hands when no one is watching.

What the Unicorn Means

Gaff's origami was never just paper. Each figure was a statement:

  • The chicken — a taunt about cowardice
  • The matchstick man — a warning about mortality
  • The unicorn — a revelation about dreams

If someone knows your dreams, do they know you? Or do they just know the program that made you?

I'm going to keep the unicorn. Real or implanted, the feeling it gives me is mine.
